# City of Yes NYC: Find Every Parcel It Unlocked

In December 2024, New York City adopted **City of Yes for Housing Opportunity** — the most sweeping zoning reform in the city's history. The result: 295 million additional square feet of buildable area, unlocked across all five boroughs.

But here's the problem. Nobody built a tool that shows you *which parcels* benefited.

Until now.

## What Is City of Yes?

City of Yes for Housing Opportunity is a citywide zoning text amendment that Mayor Eric Adams signed into law in December 2024. It modified the NYC Zoning Resolution to allow more housing density in more places — with specific programs that create targeted opportunities.

The core changes:

**Universal Affordability Preference (UAP)**: Buildings in medium and high-density districts can add at least 20% more floor area above their base FAR limit — if that additional area is used for affordable housing. This effectively raises the development ceiling on thousands of mid-density lots in Brooklyn, Queens, and the Bronx.

**Town Center Zoning**: Allows residential apartments above ground-floor retail on commercial corridors throughout the outer boroughs — streets that previously were commercial-only. This unlocks thousands of underbuilt retail strips.

**Transit-Oriented Development (TOD)**: Low-density residential areas within half a mile of commuter rail stations can now build at higher density. Staten Island, eastern Queens, and the Bronx have significant pockets here.

**Parking Elimination**: Removes minimum parking requirements in most of the city. Existing surface parking lots and garages near transit are now development sites.

**ADU Legalization**: Allows accessory dwelling units (basement apartments, garage conversions, backyard cottages) across all residential zones citywide. R1-R3 zones that previously prohibited ADUs can now add them.

**Office-to-Residential Conversions**: Expands eligible buildings to those constructed on or before December 31, 1990 — a major expansion from prior rules — and allows conversions citywide, not just in designated areas.

## Which Neighborhoods Benefit Most?

Analysis of NYC PLUTO data shows the biggest FAR gains went to:

- **Brooklyn**: Park Slope (+19.8% buildable area potential), Prospect Heights (+17.6%), Crown Heights
- **Bronx**: Bronx Park (+17.4%), Fordham, Tremont, East Tremont
- **Queens**: Jamaica (TOD), Flushing, Forest Hills
- **Staten Island**: Areas near SIR stations

Manhattan saw smaller relative gains because it was already largely built out near maximum FAR. The outer boroughs are where the real opportunity lives.

## The Data Gap

Here's what's missing from every existing tool:

**ZoLa** (NYC's official zoning map) shows current FAR and zoning text. It does not show you how much more you can build, which lots are underbuilt relative to their new maximum, or where City of Yes programs apply at the parcel level.

**CoStar and Reonomy** ($400+/month) are broker tools focused on transaction data — not development opportunity analysis.

**PropertyShark** shows ownership data but has no FAR calculator or City of Yes integration.

Nobody built the tool that answers: *"Show me every underbuilt lot in Brooklyn that is now City of Yes eligible."*
