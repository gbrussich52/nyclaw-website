**Daily cadence:**
- Morning: Send 10 emails (new batch)
- Midday: Follow up on Day 3 non-responders from earlier in week
- Afternoon: LinkedIn activity (connections + short messages)
- Evening: Review responses, book workshops, update tracking sheet

**Goals by Friday:**
- 25-30 emails sent
- 15-20 LinkedIn touches

- 2-3 free workshops booked (converts at 30-50% of replies for a good pitch)
- At least 1 workshop completed before end of week (book early in the week)

**Workshop prep for each booked call:**
- Research dealership 15 min before call
- Load their brand/location context into your demo Claude Project
- Have objection responses ready (see retainer packages doc)

**Tracking what to measure:**
- Reply rate by variant (which email is working?)

- Reply rate by title (GM vs. Sales Manager?)
- Workshop show-up rate (did they actually show?)

## WEEK 2-3 — CONDUCT PAID WORKSHOPS

**If Week 1 went right:** 1-2 paid workshops are booked for this period.

**Before each paid workshop:**
- Confirm attendance with sales manager 48 hours out (reps cancel)
- Send pre-workshop note: "Please ask your top 3-4 reps to think about one sales task they do repeatedly that feels like it should be automated. We'll use those as our starting point."
- Prep your demo Project with their brand, location, and known inventory (look at their website listings)
- Pack your one-pager and have the retainer doc ready to email

**During workshops:**
- Follow the 4-hour paid workshop outline precisely
- Take notes on every workflow they mention — these become your retainer pitch
- The best close is: "You just watched us build that in 2 hours. Imagine what we do in a month."

**After each workshop (same day):**
- Send recap email with:
  - System prompt they can copy-paste
  - How to add reps to their Claude Project
  - Retainer packages (attach as PDF)
  - Calendly link for follow-up call (book it for 3-5 days out)

**Week 2-3 parallel activity:**
- Keep sending cold emails to new prospects (10/day)
- Ask first workshop attendees for referrals — "Do you know any other dealers in [area] who would benefit from this?"
- Post a non-salesy LinkedIn update: "Just ran a 4-hour AI workshop with a dealership in Queens. The moment when their sales rep saw the call log agent output and said 'that's exactly what I'd write' — that's the thing." (Builds credibility for inbound)

## WEEK 4 — CLOSE 1-2 RETAINER CLIENTS

**Retainer close call agenda (15-30 min):**
1. "How's the team using the agent since the workshop?" (let them report success)
2. "What are the two workflows we didn't get to?" (surface the pain that's still there)
3. "Here's what the ongoing looks like — [walk through packages, start with Package B]"
4. "Which of these fits where you are right now?"
5. Handle objections (see retainer packages doc)
6. If yes: send invoice, onboarding date, Slack channel setup
7. If "not yet": set 30-day check-in, stay warm

**Close 1 client = ~$5,000/month**
**Close 2 clients = ~$10,000/month**

That's Month 1 recurring. Enough to validate the model and fund Month 2.

**Parallel: referral loop**
- First client close = ask immediately: "Do you know two other dealers who should see this?"
- Offer: "If you refer a dealer who books a workshop, I'll give you a free extra agent build."
- Auto dealer community is tight. One good reference opens 3 doors.
